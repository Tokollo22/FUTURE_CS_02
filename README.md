## Project Overview

Phishing is one of the most common cyber attacks where attackers send fraudulent emails pretending to be legitimate organizations in order to steal sensitive information such as passwords, banking details and personal data.

These emails often contain malicious links or attachments designed to trick users into revealing confidential information.

In this project, multiple email samples were analyzed to identify phishing indicators and classify them based on their risk level. The goal is to help users understand how phishing attacks work and how they can protect themselves from such threats.

---

## Project Objectives

- Identify phishing indicators in email messages
-	Analyse suspicious links and sender addresses
-	Classify emails based on risk level
-	Phishing techniques in business-friendly language
-	Educate users on how to detect phishing attacks 

---

## Tools Used

- Phishing email samples (Email examples)
- Email header analyser
- Web browser inspection tools

## Email Classification

### Email Sample 1

**Sender:** support@paypaI.com  
**Subject:** Urgent: Your PayPal Account Will Be Suspended  

#### Indicators Detected
- Fake domain name (paypaI.com instead of paypal.com)
- Suspicious external link
- Urgent language designed to create panic
- Request to verify sensitive information

#### Classification
**Phishing**

---

### Email Sample 2

**Sender:** delivery@fastcourier-service.com  
**Subject:** Delivery Failed – Update Address  

#### Indicators Detected
- Suspicious domain name
- Urgency to update personal details
- Unknown delivery service
- External link requesting personal information

#### Classification
**Phishing**

---

### Email Sample 3

**Sender:** hr@company.com  
**Subject:** Monthly Staff Meeting Reminder  

#### Indicators Detected
- Known sender domain
- No suspicious links
- Professional communication style
- No request for sensitive information

#### Classification
**Safe**

## Common Phishing Techniques

### Email Spoofing
Attackers pretend to be a trusted organization by creating fake email addresses that look similar to legitimate ones.

### Fake Websites
Phishing emails often redirect users to fake websites designed to steal login credentials and personal information.

### Malicious Links
Links in phishing emails lead users to credential-stealing pages or malicious websites.

---

## Prevention & Security Awareness Guidelines

Users can reduce phishing risks by following these security practices:

- -	Do not click suspicious links in emails.
-	Report suspicious emails to the IT/security team.
-	Never share passwords or personal information via email.
-	Hover over links to check the real website address.

---

## Conclusion

Phishing attacks are among the most common cyber threats used by attackers to trick users into revealing confidential information.

By learning how to identify phishing indicators such as fake domains, malicious links and urgent messages to users, it can significantly reduce the risk of falling victim to phishing attacks.

Security awareness and cautious email handling are essential steps in protecting both personal and organizational data.
